California Business and Professions Code Sections 20000 through 20043 provide rights to you concerning termination or non-renewal of a franchise. If the Master Franchise Agreement contains provisions that are inconsistent with the law, the law will control. 2. The Master Franchise Agreement provides for termination upon bankruptcy. This provision may not be enforceable under Federal Bankruptcy Law (11 U.S.C.A. Sec. 101 et seq. ). 3. <*$p$*>The Master Franchise Agreement contains covenants not to compete which extend for a period of 10 years beyond the termination of the agreement, within a 500-mile radius, and applies to any business involved in the preparation or sale of soup or related food products. This covenant is fully enforceable.<*$p$*> 4. Section 31125 of the California Corporation Code requires the franchisor to provide you with a disclosure document before asking you to agree to a material modification of an existing franchise. 5. Neither the franchisor, any person or franchise broker in Item 2 of the Disclosure Document is subject to any currently effective order of any national securities association or national securities exchange, as defined in the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, 15 U.S.C.A. 79a et seq., suspending or expelling such persons from membership in such association or exchange. 6. <*$p$*>The Master Franchise Agreement requires mandatory binding arbitration in New York. The franchisee is responsible for all costs associated with arbitration, including travel and legal fees. The decision of the arbitrator shall be final and non-appealable.<*$p$*> 7. The Master Franchise Agreement requires application of the laws of New York.
